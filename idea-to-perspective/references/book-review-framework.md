# Book Review Framework: 500-800 Word Structure

> Reference file for idea-to-perspective skill.

## Structural Model Derived from Published Examples

The perspective/ folder contains published academic book reviews. The student output compresses this anatomy into 500-800 words.

---

### Move 1: Hook / Problem Awareness (50-80 words)

**Function**: Establish why this book matters for the student's specific puzzle.

**Do not start with**: Book metadata dump.

**Do start with**: The student's own puzzle or a paradox in their topic that this book illuminates.

**Example** (Feng Weijiang on Kissinger):
> China's economic growth has broken almost everyone's expectations... Kissinger's On China aims to extract the basis and patterns of China's strategic behavior from traditional cultural resources.

**Template**:
> My research focuses on [core phenomenon], and I have been puzzled by [specific question]. Reading [Scholar]'s [Book], I discovered that their judgment on [type of phenomenon] precisely pierces this puzzle: [one-sentence core thesis].

---

### Move 2: Textual Summary (100-150 words)

**Function**: Accurate comprehension, not chapter-by-chapter summary.

**Key discipline**: Capture the argument in ONE sentence, then support with 2-3 key cases/concepts.

**Example** (Zeng Xueyun on New Orange Seller):
> New Orange Seller contains 11 topics and 56 essays, largely discussing truth versus prejudice... The essay Orange Seller uses transaction cost theory to verify Stigler's explanation of price discrimination.

**Template**:
> The book's core claim: [one sentence]. This rests on three arguments: first, [A]; second, [B]; third, [C]. Notably, the author emphasizes [overlooked detail].

---

### Move 3: Theoretical Grafting (150-200 words)

**Function**: THE CORE MOVE. Translate the student's topic into the book's theoretical language.

**Critical requirement**: The book's core concept must appear at least twice and actively reshape the research question.

**Example** (Feng Weijiang on Go vs Chess):
> Dr. Kissinger uses Go and chess to illustrate East-West divergence... Go does not aim to eliminate the opponent; chess does. This reframes China's strategic behavior from realist power politics to process-oriented relational logic.

**Template**:
> If we apply [core concept] to my topic [issue], my previous assumption [default] no longer holds. According to [Scholar], [phenomenon] is not [common-sense] but [new judgment]. Specifically, [concept] implies: [mechanism]. This forces me to re-ask: [new question]?

---

### Move 4: Critical Response (100-150 words)

**Function**: Genuine limitation or blind spot. Not a token sentence.

**Example** (Wang Aiyun on Vogel):
> Vogel relies heavily on oral history, which risks memory distortion... He cites an unverified web interview as fact. More fundamentally, his factional analysis framework misreads Chinese politics as zero-sum power struggle, ignoring institutional consensus.

**Template**:
> However, [Scholar]'s framework has a notable blind spot when applied to my topic: [specific limitation]. First, [empirical weakness]. Second, [conceptual oversight]. Third, [temporal or contextual misfit]. Therefore, while [concept] powerfully explains [aspect A], it cannot account for [aspect B] in my research context.

---

### Move 5: Perspective Rewrite (50-100 words)

**Function**: Rewrite the proposal's expected-finding sentence using the book's language.

**Template**:
> Rewriting my original expected finding with [Scholar]'s lens: Research expects to find that [original default] is not [surface cause] but [core concept] operating through [mechanism], which implies [policy or theoretical implication].

---

## Quality Benchmarks

| Dimension | Weight | Excellent | Adequate | Inadequate |
|:----------|:-------|:----------|:---------|:-----------|
| Textual understanding | 20% | Core argument in one sentence; key evidence identified | Chapter summary | Misrepresents thesis |
| Theoretical grafting | 35% | Concept appears 2+ times; actively reshapes RQ | Concept mentioned once | Concept floating/unconnected |
| Critical depth | 25% | Specific limitation with evidence | Generic limitation | No criticism / only praise |
| Perspective rewrite | 20% | Original finding transformed, not just reworded | Minor wording change | Unchanged from original |

## Anti-Patterns to Flag

1. **Book report trap**: Spends 80% of words summarizing chapters, 20% on analysis. Fix: Cap summary at 150 words.
2. **Concept sticker**: Says 'this book's X concept is very relevant' without showing HOW it changes the causal story. Fix: Force mechanism narration.
3. **Praise-only syndrome**: 'This book is groundbreaking, insightful, and profound.' Fix: Require one concrete disagreement.
4. **Generic hook**: 'With the development of society, more and more people pay attention to...' Fix: Start from personal puzzle.
5. **No page citation**: Claims to have read book but cites no specific passage. Fix: Require direct quote + page number.
